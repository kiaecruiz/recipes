Adobo

Chicken adobo is a cuisine that is traditional and wholly Pinoy, and it is adored by many people all across the nation. Although there have been many other adobo dishes throughout the years, chicken adobo is still one of the most well-liked, and for good reason! Chicken adobo is a comfort food that appeals to people of all ages due to the taste of juicy chicken in a luscious, umami adobo sauce. Kids and adults alike can't get enough of this delectable feast!

Ingredients

  • 2 tbps vegetable oil
  • 3 lbs of chicken cut into pieces
  • 1 large onion, quarteres and slices
  • 2 tbps minced garlic
  • 2/3 cup low sodium soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup white vinegar
  • 1 tbps garlic powder
  • 2 tps black pepper
  • 1 bay leaf

Directions

  1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook chicken pieces until golden brown, 2-3 mins. per side. TRansfer chicken to a plate and set aside.
  2. Add onion and garlic to the skillet; cook until softened and brown, about 6 minutes. Pour in soy sauce and vinegar and season with garlic powder, black pepper, and bay leaf.
  3. Return chicken to pan, increase heat to high, and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until chicken is tender and cooked through, 35 to 40 minutes.
